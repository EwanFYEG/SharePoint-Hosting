Scenario for AI Use Case Proritisation Framework

Green Grain Foods Ltd, is a mid-szed food manufacturing company expereincing operational inefficiencies and increased compettion in the market. They have identified a number of potental AI use cases but lack a structured way to evaluate, prioritise and sequence them for delivery.

The Prioritization Matrix

Each AI initiative is assessed using the following six key criteria, with weights reflecting their strategic importance:

Criteria Description Weighting
Business Value/Impact Evaluates potential for revenue growth, cost reductions, market differentiation, and enhanced customer experience. 25%
Strategic Alignment Assesses how well the initiative supports the company’s long-term vision and competitive positioning. 20%
Technical Feasibility Considers implementation complexity, resource availability, and integration with existing systems. 15%
Data Readiness & Quality Reviews whether the necessary data is available, clean, and ready to power the AI solution. 15%
Cost & Risk Assessment Analyzes expected costs, ongoing expenditures, and potential risks, including regulatory and scalability concerns. 15%
Time-to-Value Estimates the deployment timeline and the time needed to achieve measurable benefits. 10%

Rationale Behind the Framework

This balanced framework bridges strategic vision with actionable execution:

  • Vision & Impact: By emphasizing Business Value and Strategic Alignment, we ensure that each initiative not only drives revenue but also fits our market position.
  • Practical Execution: Technical Feasibility and Data Readiness ensure projects are grounded in our current infrastructure and data capabilities.
  • Risk & Reward: Cost & Risk Assessment and Time-to-Value guarantee that we focus on initiatives with manageable risks while also capturing early wins.

Each initiative is scored on a 1–5 scale for every criterion. Multiplying these scores by the respective weightings produces a composite score that objectively ranks our opportunities. Visual aids such as heat maps or quadrant charts further illustrate immediate “quick wins” versus more strategic, long-term bets.

This dynamic framework encourages informed, transparent decision-making. It continuously evolves with market changes, new data, and internal resource shifts—ensuring we consistently align AI initiatives with both immediate needs and long-term growth.
